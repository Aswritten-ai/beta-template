## [Terminology Reference]

### Key Phrases
*   **Collective Memory:** The total sum of institutional knowledge made machine-readable.
*   **The Witness:** The AI agent/process responsible for capturing and structuring memories.
*   **Observe vs. Witness:** Observing is passive data collection; witnessing is the active process of clarifying intent and committing it to the graph.[^term-witness]
*   **Commit to the Witness:** The act of finalizing a memory into the version-controlled graph.
*   **One worldview, aligned output:** The state where all agents (human and AI) operate from the same factual and strategic basis.

### Verbs
*   **Observe:** To monitor organizational activity for potential memories.
*   **Witness:** To validate and structure a memory with human input.
*   **Commit:** To save a structured memory to the Git-native graph.
*   **Query:** To retrieve context from the graph for AI generation.

### Metaphors
*   **Git for your Brain:** Version-controlled institutional knowledge.
*   **The Organizational Soul:** The intangible "vibe" and logic that Collective Memory makes tangible for AI.
